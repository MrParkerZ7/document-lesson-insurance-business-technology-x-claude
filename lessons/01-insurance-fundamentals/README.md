# Lesson 01: Insurance Business Fundamentals

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:
- Understand what insurance is and how it works

- Explain the insurance value chain
- Understand the insurance business model and revenue streams

---

## 1.1 What is Insurance?

Insurance is a **risk transfer mechanism** where an individual or entity (policyholder) pays a premium to an insurance company (insurer) in exchange for financial protection against potential future losses.

### Core Principles

| Principle | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Utmost Good Faith** | Both parties must disclose all material facts |
| **Insurable Interest** | Policyholder must have financial stake in the insured |
| **Indemnity** | Compensation limited to actual loss (non-life) |
| **Subrogation** | Insurer's right to recover from third party |
| **Contribution** | Multiple insurers share the loss proportionally |
| **Proximate Cause** | Nearest cause of loss determines coverage |

## 1.2 Key Stakeholders

| Stakeholder | Role | Responsibilities |
|-------------|------|------------------|
| **Insurer** | The insurance company that underwrites and issues policies | Risk assessment, policy issuance, claims payment |
| **Policyholder** | Individual or entity that purchases insurance | Premium payment, claim submission, disclosure |
| **Agent/Broker** | Intermediary who sells insurance products | Sales, customer service, policy servicing |
| **Underwriter** | Assesses risk and determines policy terms | Risk evaluation, pricing, acceptance decisions |
| **Claims Adjuster** | Evaluates and processes claims | Investigation, assessment, settlement |
| **Actuary** | Analyzes statistical data to calculate premiums | Pricing models, reserve calculations, risk analysis |
| **Regulator** | Government body overseeing insurance industry | Licensing, compliance, consumer protection |
| **Reinsurer** | Insures insurance companies against large losses | Risk sharing, capacity provision |

## 1.4 Insurance Business Model

### Revenue Streams

| Revenue Type | Description | Example |
|--------------|-------------|---------|
| **Premium Income** | Money collected from policyholders | Annual motor premium of $1,200 |
| **Investment Income** | Returns from investing premium reserves | Interest on bonds, stock dividends |
| **Fee Income** | Administrative fees, policy fees | Policy issuance fee of $50 |
| **Commission Income** | For agents/brokers selling policies | 15% commission on premium |

### Cost Structure

| Cost Type | Description | Typical % of Premium |
|-----------|-------------|---------------------|
| **Claims Payout** | Largest expense (Loss Ratio) | 60-80% |
| **Operating Expenses** | Salaries, technology, marketing | 15-25% |
| **Commissions** | Paid to agents/brokers | 5-20% |
| **Reinsurance Costs** | Premium paid to reinsurers | 5-15% |

### Key Financial Metrics

| Metric | Formula | Target | Description |
|--------|---------|--------|-------------|
| **Loss Ratio** | Claims Paid / Premiums Earned | < 70% | Measures claims efficiency |
| **Expense Ratio** | Operating Expenses / Premiums Written | < 25% | Measures operational efficiency |
| **Combined Ratio** | Loss Ratio + Expense Ratio | < 100% | < 100% = Underwriting profit |
| **Solvency Ratio** | Assets / Liabilities | > 150% | Measures financial health |
| **Retention Ratio** | Renewed Policies / Expiring Policies | > 85% | Customer loyalty indicator |

## 1.5 Distribution Channels

| Channel | Description | Pros | Cons |
|---------|-------------|------|------|
| **Tied Agents** | Exclusive agents for one insurer | Brand loyalty, trained | Limited products |
| **Independent Brokers** | Represent multiple insurers | Product variety | Split loyalty |
| **Bancassurance** | Sold through banks | Large customer base | Limited expertise |
| **Direct/Online** | Sold via website/app | Low cost, 24/7 | Limited advice |
| **Aggregators** | Comparison websites | Price transparency | Commoditization |
| **Affinity Groups** | Group policies via associations | Built-in market | Niche coverage |

---

## Key Takeaways

1. Insurance is fundamentally about **risk transfer** and **pooling**
2. Multiple stakeholders work together in the insurance ecosystem
3. The value chain spans from product design to claims settlement
4. Profitability depends on balancing premiums, claims, and expenses
5. Distribution channels are evolving with digital transformation
