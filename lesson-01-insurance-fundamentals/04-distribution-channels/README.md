# 01.4 Distribution Channels

## Overview

Distribution channels are the pathways through which insurance products reach customers. Understanding these channels is crucial for designing systems that support multi-channel sales and service.

## Channel Types

| Channel | Description | Pros | Cons |
|---------|-------------|------|------|
| **Tied Agents** | Exclusive agents for one insurer | Brand loyalty, trained | Limited products |
| **Independent Brokers** | Represent multiple insurers | Product variety | Split loyalty |
| **Bancassurance** | Sold through banks | Large customer base | Limited expertise |
| **Direct/Online** | Sold via website/app | Low cost, 24/7 | Limited advice |
| **Aggregators** | Comparison websites | Price transparency | Commoditization |
| **Affinity Groups** | Group policies via associations | Built-in market | Niche coverage |

## Digital Transformation

### Traditional vs Digital

| Aspect | Traditional | Digital |
|--------|-------------|---------|
| **Availability** | Business hours | 24/7/365 |
| **Quote Time** | Hours/Days | Minutes |
| **Documentation** | Paper-based | Electronic |
| **Customer Data** | Manual entry | Auto-capture |
| **Payment** | Check/Cash | Online payment |
| **Policy Delivery** | Mail | Email/Portal |

## System Requirements

### Multi-Channel Support
- Unified customer view across channels
- Channel-specific pricing rules
- Commission calculation by channel
- Lead management and distribution

### Partner Integration
- Agent/Broker portals
- Bank system integration
- Aggregator API connectivity
- White-label solutions

### Analytics Needs
- Channel performance metrics
- Conversion rate tracking
- Cost per acquisition
- Customer journey analysis
